Major Historical Events and Figures
Ancient History

Mesopotamia (c. 3500–539 BCE): Birthplace of writing (cuneiform), the wheel, and early city-states.

Ancient Egypt: Construction of pyramids, pharaohs like Ramses II, and development of hieroglyphics.

Indus Valley Civilization (c. 3300–1300 BCE): Urban planning, trade, and early sanitation systems in Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro.

Ancient Greece: Birth of democracy, philosophy (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle), and the Olympic Games.

Roman Empire: Roman law, engineering (aqueducts, roads), Julius Caesar, Augustus, and the spread of Christianity.

Medieval History

Byzantine Empire: Preservation of Roman culture, Hagia Sophia, and Justinian Code.

Feudal Europe: Rise of kingdoms, knights, and castles; the Black Death (1347–1351).

Islamic Golden Age: Scientific, mathematical, and cultural advances; scholars like Al-Khwarizmi and Avicenna.

Mongol Empire: Genghis Khan’s expansion, trade along the Silk Road.

Indian Medieval Period: Delhi Sultanate, Mughal Empire, Akbar the Great, and cultural synthesis.

Early Modern History

Renaissance (14th–17th Century): Revival of arts, literature, and science in Europe; Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Galileo.

Reformation (16th Century): Martin Luther, religious reform, and the rise of Protestantism.

Age of Exploration: Christopher Columbus, Vasco da Gama, and Ferdinand Magellan; global trade and colonization.

Scientific Revolution: Isaac Newton, Galileo Galilei, Copernicus, and advances in physics, astronomy, and biology.

Modern History

Industrial Revolution (18th–19th Century): Mechanization, steam engines, factories, and urbanization.

American Revolution (1775–1783): Independence from Britain; figures: George Washington, Thomas Jefferson.

French Revolution (1789–1799): Liberty, equality, fraternity; fall of monarchy; Napoleon Bonaparte.

World War I (1914–1918): Causes: alliances, imperialism; trench warfare; Treaty of Versailles.

Russian Revolution (1917): Fall of Tsarist Russia; rise of Lenin and the Soviet Union.

World War II (1939–1945): Hitler, Nazi Germany, Holocaust, Allies vs Axis, Hiroshima and Nagasaki, UN formation.

Indian Independence Movement: Mahatma Gandhi, Salt March (Dandi March, 1930), Quit India Movement, independence in 1947.

Civil Rights Movement (1950s–1960s, USA): Martin Luther King Jr., Rosa Parks, desegregation, equality movements.

Key Historical Figures

Mahatma Gandhi: Leader of India’s independence, non-violent resistance.

Adolf Hitler: German dictator, instigated World War II, Holocaust.

Winston Churchill: British PM during WWII, known for leadership and speeches.

Abraham Lincoln: 16th US President, abolished slavery, led through the Civil War.

Napoleon Bonaparte: French military leader, emperor, legal reforms (Napoleonic Code).

Albert Einstein: Physicist, theory of relativity, contributions to modern science.

Cleopatra: Last Pharaoh of Egypt, diplomacy, and political acumen.

Julius Caesar: Roman general, dictator, expanded Roman territories, assassinated 44 BCE.

Important Movements & Innovations

Renaissance & Humanism: Focus on arts, education, and human potential.

Scientific Revolution & Enlightenment: Reason, logic, and evidence-based thinking.

Industrialization: Factories, urban growth, mechanized labor.

Decolonization: Post-WWII independence movements across Asia and Africa.

Technological Innovations: Printing press, electricity, steam engines, internet.
